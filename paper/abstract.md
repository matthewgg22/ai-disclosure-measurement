# Abstract (draft)

*Working title:* **The Decoupling of the "AI" Label from Substance in US Securities
Filings, 2001–2025.**

In US 10-K filings, the phrase "artificial intelligence" spread from 0.8% of filers
in 2001 to 50.7% in 2025. Over the same period, cheap marketing vocabulary
("AI-powered", "AI-driven") reached 14.6% of filers while costly build vocabulary
(large language model, training compute, fine-tuning) stayed near 1.8%. Software
(SIC-73) fell from a ~57% (2018) peak share of AI mentions to 27% (2025) as the label
diffused across sectors. Measuring capability with audited R&D, the R&D-intensity
premium of AI-labeled filers over the market compressed toward zero across 2018–2024
(+0.036 → +0.009) even as the AI-labeled pool grew from 37 firms (2015) to ~2,400
(2024); the 2015 benchmark is a small sample (n=37) and is flagged as such. Together
these describe a label whose information content about real capability decayed as it
proliferated — a lemons precondition — measured entirely at the market level from
public data, with no claim about any individual issuer.
